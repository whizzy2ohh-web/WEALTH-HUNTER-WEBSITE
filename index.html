The Foundation of Longevity

Risk Management: The Edge Most Traders Ignore

At Hunters Academy, risk management isn't an afterthought it's the foundation of every trade we take. Consistent profitability is 70% discipline and 30% strategy. Here's our complete framework.

Position Sizing

Never risk more than 12% of your account on a single trade. We use dynamic lot sizing formulas based on account balance, stop-loss distance, and market volatility to keep every trade within safe parameters.

Stop Loss Discipline

A trade without a stop loss isn't a trade it's a gamble. We teach you to place stops at logical structural levels, not arbitrary pip counts, so price needs to truly invalidate your thesis before exiting.

Risk-to-Reward Ratio

We only take trades with a minimum 1:2 RRR. This means even with a 40% win rate, you remain profitable. Hunting 1:3 and 1:5 setups is how small accounts grow exponentially over time.

Trading Psychology

Fear and greed destroy more accounts than bad analysis. We integrate psychological frameworks, journaling protocols, and mental conditioning routines into our curriculum to build disciplined minds.

Drawdown Management

We set hard daily loss limits (5%) and weekly drawdown caps (10%). When these are hit, we stop no exceptions. Protecting capital is the primary directive. Growth follows naturally from survival.

Trade Journaling

Every trade gets logged entry, exit, rationale, emotion, outcome. Your journal is your feedback loop. Over time, patterns emerge that tell you exactly where your edge is strongest and where your biases hurt you.

Our Core Rules

The 10 Commandments of the Wealth Hunter

01

Never risk more than 1% per trade

Account preservation is the first law. A 50% drawdown requires a 100% gain just to break even. Small consistent risks compound into large consistent returns.

02

Always place your stop loss before your entry

Know your invalidation point before you enter. If you can't identify a logical stop, you don't have a trade you have a hope.

03

Minimum 1:2 RRR no exceptions

If the market structure doesn't offer room for at least 2R, the trade doesn't meet our criteria regardless of how confident you feel about the direction.

04

Stop after 2 consecutive losses

Two losses in a row signals the market may not be aligned with your analysis. Walk away, review your trades, and return with a clear head the next session.

05

Never move your stop loss to accommodate price

Moving your stop is emotional trading. If price reaches your stop, the thesis is invalid. Accept it and move on. The market owes you nothing.

06

Trade the plan, not the emotion

Your trading plan was written with a clear mind. Your emotions during a live trade are not your ally. Trust the analysis, execute the plan, detach from the outcome.

07

Review your journal weekly, not just your P&L

Profit numbers hide the truth. Your journal reveals it. Patterns in your decision-making good and bad only appear when you review consistently.

08

Size down during losing streaks, size up during winning ones

Anti-martingale principles keep you in the game. Increase size when performance confirms your edge is working. Reduce when the market is fighting you.

09

Respect high-impact news events

NFP, FOMC, CPI these events create erratic volatility that invalidates even the best technical setups. Either close positions before or stay out entirely.

10

Consistency beats perfection every time

A trader with 55% win rate and strict 1:2 RRR will outperform the trader chasing 90% win rates with poor risk control. Show up every day, follow the rules, trust the process.
